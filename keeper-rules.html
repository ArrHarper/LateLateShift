Keeper Rules

Last Updated: 8/20/23

General

  • Up to 2 keepers per team. Keeping players is optional.
  • Keeper selection deadline is 24 hours prior to Draft Time.

Keeper Eligibility

  • Players can be kept for 2 consecutive seasons after their draft year, then returned to the draft pool.
  • Players drafted in the 1st round are are ineligible and cannot be kept. This keeps a healthy pool of elite players in the draft mix each year.

Cost to Keep Drafted Players

  • Keeper cost is equal to the playerâ€™s most recent draft position, e.g. a player selected in round 4 in 2021 costs a 4th round pick to keep in 2022.
  • Keeping players for a second consecutive season increases the cost by one round, so a 4th round selection costs a 3rd round pick to keep a second time.

Example

Mark Andrews was drafted 6th round 2020.
Andrews costs a 6th rd pick to keep in 2021,
Andrews costs a 5th rd pick to keep in 2022.
In 2023, Andrews is ineligible and returns to the draft pool.

Cost to Keep Undrafted Free Agents (UDFAs)

  • Undrafted Free Agents (UDFA) are players not drafted by any team in the most recent draft.
  • Keeping a UDFA costs an 8th round pick for the first year.
  • Keeping a UDFA costs a 7th round pick for the second year.

Example

Justin Jefferson was signed as an UDFA in 2020.
Jefferson costs a 8th rd pick to keep in 2021,
Jefferson costs a 7th rd pick to keep in 2022.
In 2023, Jefferson is ineligible and returns to the draft pool.

FAQ

What if I want to keep two UDFAs, or two players drafted in the same round?

In this situation, the cost for the second keeper increases by one round. So keeping 2 UDFAs costs an 8th and 7th round pick. Keeping two players drafted in the 10th round costs a 10th and a 9th round pick.


If I only keep a player for one season, can I pick him again in the draft?

Yes, if you return a player to the draft pool and have the opportunity to draft them back onto your roster, you can do this and the keeper costs will reset.
